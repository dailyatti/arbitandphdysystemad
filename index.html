The Mechanics of Arbitrage

An arbitrage opportunity ("surebet") arises when the sum of the implied probabilities for all possible outcomes of an event is strictly less than 1 (or 100%). By strategically covering all outcomes with the correct stake allocation, an investor guarantees a return regardless of the event's result.

Condition for Arbitrage

$$ \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{O_i} < 1 $$

Where \( O_i \) represents the decimal odds for outcome \( i \), and \( n \) is the total number of mutually exclusive outcomes.

Optimal Stake Allocation

To mathematically distribute our bankroll such that the profit is perfectly balanced across any outcome, our software uses the inverse proportion of the odds against the sum of implied probabilities.

Stake Formula

$$ S_i = \frac{T \times \frac{1}{O_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^{n} \frac{1}{O_j}} $$

Where \( S_i \) is the stake for outcome \( i \), and \( T \) is your Total Investment Bankroll.

Practical Derivation Example

Imagine a Tennis match with two outcomes (Player A wins, Player B wins) offered by two different bookmakers:

  • Bookmaker 1: Player A to win at odds \( O_A = 2.10 \)
  • Bookmaker 2: Player B to win at odds \( O_B = 2.15 \)
$$ \text{Arbitrage Margin} = \frac{1}{2.10} + \frac{1}{2.15} = 0.4761 + 0.4651 = 0.9412 $$

Since \( 0.9412 < 1 \), this is a risk-free arbitrage opportunity. With a $1,000 Total Stake (\( T \)):

$$ S_A = \frac{1000 \times 0.4761}{0.9412} = \$505.84 \quad | \quad S_B = \frac{1000 \times 0.4651}{0.9412} = \$494.16 $$
Guaranteed Payout: \( \$505.84 \times 2.10 = \$1062.26 \) A guaranteed 6.22% Return on Investment (ROI).

Expected Value Calculation

The cornerstone of the PhD paradigm is betting exclusively when there is positive Expected Value (+EV). Over a large sample size, variance converges to the mathematical expectation.

Value Valuation

$$ EV = (P_{win} \times V_{profit}) - (P_{lose} \times S) $$

Alternatively expressed as a percentage edge: \( \text{Edge} = (P_{win} \times O) - 1 > 0 \)

Bankroll Management: Fractional Kelly

Maximizing geometric growth rate while minimizing the risk of ruin (drawdown). The PhD system relies on a dynamically adjusting Fractional Kelly Criterion.

Kelly Formula

$$ f^* = c \left( \frac{bp - q}{b} \right) $$

Where \( b \) is the net odds received (Decimal Odds - 1), \( p \) is probability of win, \( q \) is \( 1-p \), and \( c \) is the fractional multiplier (e.g., 0.25).
